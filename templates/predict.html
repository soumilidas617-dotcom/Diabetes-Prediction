Our predictive model relies on a curated set of eight crucial biometric and historical variables, primarily informed by the Pima Indian Diabetes Dataset. We employ proprietary weighting algorithms to maximize the predictive power of each factor.

💜

Metabolic Health (Glucose & Insulin)

These variables are direct indicators of metabolic function and the body's ability to regulate blood sugar. High plasma glucose or dysregulated insulin levels are key precursors to Type 2 Diabetes.

📈

Anthropometric Indicators (BMI & Age)

Body Mass Index (BMI) measures adiposity, a major risk factor, while Age reflects cumulative exposure to risk factors and metabolic decline. They are critical for assessing predisposing physical conditions.

🧬

Genetic & Historical Risk (Pedigree & Pregnancies)

The Diabetes Pedigree Function statistically assesses genetic predisposition based on family history. Pregnancies (for females) accounts for gestational diabetes history, a powerful future risk predictor.

🔮

Vascular & Physical Measures (BP & Skin Fold)

Blood Pressure is crucial as hypertension often co-occurs with diabetes. Skin Fold Thickness (a proxy for body fat distribution) provides supplementary data for a more holistic physical assessment.
